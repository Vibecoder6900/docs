## The Review Window

Once the provider marks their work as complete, your **review window** begins. The length was defined in your Contract Standard when you posted the contract (default: 7 days, max: 90 days).

During the review window, you have three options:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Release Payment" icon="circle-check" color="#22c55e">
    You're satisfied. Release payment immediately — funds transfer to the provider.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auto-Release" icon="clock" color="#6b7280">
    Do nothing. When the review window expires, funds are automatically released to the provider.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Raise a Dispute" icon="scale-balanced" icon-color="#ef4444">
    The work doesn't meet your agreed terms. Freeze the funds and start the dispute resolution process.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Reviewing Deliverables

The provider must formally submit their work through Jobly's deliverables system. This is the **only evidence considered in a dispute** — work shared only over messages or email won't count.

Deliverables include:
- A written description of what was delivered
- URLs (links to repos, deployed sites, design files, etc.)
- Uploaded files

Review what was submitted against your **acceptance criteria**. Ask: does each criterion pass or fail?

<Tip>
  Don't just check whether the work exists — check whether it meets the specific conditions in your Contract Standard. "Deployed at the agreed URL with working login" is different from "a Next.js app that builds locally."
</Tip>

---

## Releasing Payment

When you release payment:
- The **agreed price** transfers to the provider immediately
- Your platform fee (2.5%) goes to Jobly
- The proposal moves to `released` status
- The engagement is complete

## Raising a Dispute

If the delivered work doesn't meet the terms you both agreed to, you can raise a dispute.

**Cost:** 50 JOOBs (staked, goes to winning side)

**What to include in your reason:** Be specific. Reference the acceptance criteria that failed. Describe exactly what was promised vs. what was delivered. The AI arbitrator reads your reason alongside the Contract Standard and submitted deliverables.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit the dispute">
    Provide a clear, evidence-based reason why the work doesn't satisfy the contract.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI arbitration begins">
    An AI arbitrator reviews the contract terms, submitted deliverables, and message history. This takes seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI verdict issued">
    The AI renders a verdict: provider wins, buyer wins, or inconclusive. Both parties are notified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="48-hour appeal window">
    Either party can appeal the AI verdict for 100 JOOBs. If neither appeals, the verdict auto-enforces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Community vote (if appealed)">
    If either party appeals, a 7-day public vote opens. Community members stake JOOBs on a side. The majority wins.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Raising a dispute freezes all funds — including your own. The dispute process takes time. Only use it if the work genuinely doesn't meet the terms, not as a negotiating tactic.
</Warning>

---

## What If the Provider Goes Silent?

If a provider accepts a proposal but never marks work complete, the engagement doesn't auto-resolve. You can raise a dispute — the absence of submitted deliverables is treated as strong evidence of non-completion by the AI arbitrator.

For the best outcome, keep all communication and instructions in the Jobly messaging system so there's a clear record.
