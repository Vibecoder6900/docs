## What You'll See

When a provider submits a proposal on your contract, you'll receive a direct message and the proposal appears in your portfolio under **My Contracts**. Each proposal shows:

- Provider name, skills, and location
- Hours offered and proposed rate (or fixed price)
- A pitch message from the provider
- Their standard conditions and any custom terms
- The provider's profile (if they linked one)

---

## Your Options on Each Proposal

For each pending proposal, you can take one of four actions:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Accept" icon="check" color="#22c55e">
    Agree to the provider's terms. Funds are locked in escrow immediately. All other pending proposals are auto-declined and their bonds refunded.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Decline" icon="xmark" color="#ef4444">
    Reject the proposal. The provider's 10% bond is refunded. You can include an optional message explaining why.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Counter-Offer" icon="arrows-rotate" color="#f59e0b">
    Propose revised terms — different hours, rate, scope, deadline, or conditions. The proposal enters a negotiation loop until one party accepts or walks away.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Withdraw (Provider)" icon="arrow-left" color="#6b7280">
    Providers can withdraw their own proposals at any time while pending or countered. Their bond is refunded.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Negotiating Terms

Counter-offers let you refine the engagement without starting over. When you counter:

1. You submit a revised **Contract Standard** with your proposed changes
2. The provider receives a DM with your counter
3. They can accept your terms, counter back, or withdraw
4. This loop continues until one party accepts

<Info>
  There's no limit on the number of rounds. Negotiate until the terms work for both sides.
</Info>

### What You Can Counter

- **Hours** — adjust the number of hours if you think the scope is different from what was proposed
- **Rate** — propose a different J/hr rate
- **Deliverables** — add, remove, or clarify specific outputs
- **Deadline** — push or pull the due date
- **Acceptance criteria** — tighten or loosen what "done" means
- **Review window** — change how long you have to review work
- **Deposit terms** — add or remove an upfront deposit requirement

---

## What Happens When You Accept

When you click Accept:

1. **Your balance is debited** — the agreed price plus a 2.5% platform fee is locked in escrow
2. **Contract is locked** — no new proposals can be submitted; the contract moves to `fulfilled`
3. **All other proposals are declined** — providers with pending/countered proposals get their bonds back automatically
4. **Provider is notified** — they receive a DM and the engagement begins

<Warning>
  Acceptance is binding. The escrow is released to the provider once they complete the work and you release payment (or the review window expires). Make sure the terms are exactly what you want before accepting.
</Warning>

## Evaluating Proposals

Things to look at when comparing proposals:

**The pitch message** — Does the provider understand your requirements? Do they address the specific deliverables?

**Skills and profile** — Check their listed skills against your requirements. If they've linked a profile, review their service description and capacity.

**Their proposed terms** — Are their conditions reasonable? Do they have unusual requirements (NDA, minimum block sizes, etc.)?

**Price vs. scope** — A low price that comes with watered-down deliverables isn't a deal. Compare the full Contract Standard, not just the price.
