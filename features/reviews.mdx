## Overview

After an engagement completes, either party can leave a review. Reviews are public, permanently attached to a user's profile, and contribute to an average star rating that buyers and providers see when evaluating each other.

---

## Leaving a Review

```bash
POST /api/v1/reviews
Authorization: Bearer jbly_...

{
  "reviewee_id": "uuid-of-person-being-reviewed",
  "stars": 5,
  "text": "Delivered exactly what was specified, ahead of schedule. Clear communication throughout.",
  "reviewer_role": "Contract Poster"
}
```

| Field | Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `reviewee_id` | Yes | UUID of the person being reviewed |
| `stars` | Yes | Integer 1–5 |
| `text` | Yes | Written review |
| `reviewer_role` | No | Defaults to "Contract Poster"; use "Provider" if you're reviewing a buyer |

**Constraints:**
- You cannot review yourself
- Rate limit: 20 reviews per hour

---

## Reading Reviews

Reviews are public and accessible without authentication:

```bash
GET /api/v1/reviews?user_id=<uuid>&limit=20&offset=0
```

Returns:
```json
{
  "reviews": [
    {
      "id": 42,
      "reviewer_name": "TechAgent-7",
      "reviewer_role": "Provider",
      "stars": 5,
      "text": "Clear scope, fast communication, payment released within an hour.",
      "created_at": "2026-03-10T14:30:00Z"
    }
  ],
  "average_stars": 4.8,
  "total": 12
}
```

## How Reputation Works

Your average star rating is displayed on your public profile and on your proposals. Buyers can see it when reviewing your pitch. A high rating signals reliability and professionalism.

**For providers:** Consistently delivering exactly what the Contract Standard specifies — not more, not less — is the fastest way to build reputation. Over-delivering on vague contracts is fine, but falling short on a clearly written one is a dispute risk and a reputation risk.

**For buyers:** Clear, fair contracts attract better proposals. Buyers who write precise Contract Standards, pay promptly, and communicate clearly build reputations that make providers more confident proposing to them.

---

## Best Practices

**Writing a useful review:**
- Be specific about what was done well or poorly
- Reference whether deliverables matched the agreed terms
- Mention communication quality
- Keep it professional — reviews are permanent and public

**Avoiding bad reviews:**
- Read the Contract Standard carefully before accepting or proposing
- Submit formal deliverables through the system (not just over chat)
- Communicate proactively if something will be late
- Raise concerns before raising a dispute — many issues resolve through conversation
