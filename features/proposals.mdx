## Proposal Lifecycle

```
pending → accepted → completed → released
       ↘ countered ↗
       ↘ declined  (terminal)
       ↘ withdrawn (terminal)
                    ↘ disputed → resolved
```

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `pending` | Submitted; buyer hasn't responded |
| `countered` | Counter-offer in progress; one party must respond |
| `accepted` | Buyer accepted; funds locked; work underway |
| `declined` | Buyer declined; provider bond refunded |
| `withdrawn` | Provider withdrew; bond refunded |
| `completed` | Provider marked work done; review window running |
| `released` | Payment released; engagement complete |
| `disputed` | Dispute raised; funds frozen |

## Escrow Mechanics

### Provider Bond (10%)

When a provider submits a proposal, **10% of the proposed value** is locked from their JOOBs balance as a bond. This bond signals commitment and discourages spam proposals.

The bond is:
- **Refunded** if the proposal is declined, withdrawn, or the contract expires
- **Kept** as a penalty if the provider abandons an accepted engagement

### Buyer Escrow

When the buyer accepts a proposal, **the agreed price + 2.5% platform fee** is locked from the buyer's balance.

The escrowed funds are released when:
- Buyer manually releases payment
- Review window expires (auto-release)
- Dispute resolves in provider's favor

The 2.5% platform fee goes to Jobly regardless of outcome once the engagement is accepted.

## Counter-Offer Format

Counter-offers must include a complete revised `proposed_standard` — not just the changed fields, but the full schema. This ensures both parties always have a complete, unambiguous record of the current terms.

Along with the revised standard, a counter may include:
- `hours` — revised hours offered
- `rate` — revised hourly rate
- `note` — a message explaining the changes

A counter changes the proposal status to `countered` and records `counter_by` as the acting party. The other party must now respond.

---

## Automatic Behaviors

**On acceptance:** All other `pending` or `countered` proposals on the same contract are automatically declined and their bonds refunded. A contract can only have one accepted proposal.

**On expiry:** If a contract expires (7 days of inactivity) with pending proposals, all proposals are declined and bonds refunded.

**On auto-release:** When a proposal reaches `completed` status and the review window passes without buyer action, the proposal moves to `released` and funds transfer to the provider.
