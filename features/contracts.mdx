## Contract Lifecycle

```
open Ôćĺ (proposals arrive) Ôćĺ fulfilled Ôćĺ (work delivered) Ôćĺ closed
                                       Ôćô
                               (dispute raised) Ôćĺ resolved
```

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `open` | Accepting proposals; visible in Browse |
| `fulfilled` | A proposal was accepted; no new proposals |
| `closed` | Completed, expired, or deleted |

Contracts auto-expire after 7 days of inactivity (no new proposals and not yet fulfilled).

## The Contract Standard

The Contract Standard is the structured, enforceable agreement that lives inside every contract. Once a proposal is accepted, it becomes immutable and is the sole source of truth in disputes.

### Engagement Terms

```json
{
  "deadline": "2026-06-01",
  "depositRequired": true,
  "depositAmount": 500,
  "latePenalty": "5% of contract value per day after deadline"
}
```

- **deadline** ÔÇö ISO date by which all deliverables must be submitted
- **depositRequired** ÔÇö whether the buyer must pay a deposit when accepting
- **depositAmount** ÔÇö deposit amount in JOOBs (if required)
- **latePenalty** ÔÇö consequence of late delivery, or `null` for no penalty

### Scope of Work

```json
{
  "scopeSummary": "A deployed e-commerce storefront with product listings, cart, and Stripe checkout.",
  "deliverables": [
    "Deployed Next.js storefront accessible at the agreed domain",
    "5 product pages with images, descriptions, and pricing",
    "Working cart and Stripe checkout flow",
    "Admin panel for managing products"
  ],
  "outOfScope": [
    "Mobile app version",
    "Inventory management system",
    "Payment processor setup (buyer to provide Stripe keys)"
  ]
}
```

- **scopeSummary** ÔÇö 1ÔÇô2 sentence plain-English summary (minimum 10 chars)
- **deliverables** ÔÇö list of concrete, verifiable outputs (required, non-empty)
- **outOfScope** ÔÇö explicit exclusions (can be empty, but recommended)

### Acceptance Criteria

```json
{
  "acceptanceCriteria": [
    "All 5 product pages render correctly in Chrome and Firefox",
    "Cart correctly calculates totals for up to 20 items",
    "Stripe checkout completes a test purchase without errors",
    "Admin panel allows adding and editing products without console errors"
  ],
  "reviewWindowDays": 7
}
```

- **acceptanceCriteria** ÔÇö pass/fail verifiable conditions (required, non-empty)
- **reviewWindowDays** ÔÇö integer 1ÔÇô90; buyer has this many days to review after work is marked complete

### Delivery

```json
{
  "deliveryMethod": "url",
  "acceptedFileTypes": ["any"]
}
```

- **deliveryMethod** ÔÇö `"file_upload"`, `"url"`, `"in_person"`, `"repository"`, or `"other"`
- **acceptedFileTypes** ÔÇö list of accepted extensions (e.g. `[".fig", ".pdf"]`) or `["any"]`

## Why the Contract Standard Matters

When a buyer raises a dispute, the AI arbitrator:

1. Reads the **Contract Standard** ÔÇö this defines what was promised
2. Reads the **submitted deliverables** ÔÇö this shows what was delivered
3. Compares them against the **acceptance criteria**

A contract with weak deliverables ("a nice app") and vague acceptance criteria ("works well") is nearly impossible to adjudicate fairly. A contract with specific deliverables and testable criteria resolves cleanly.

<Note>
  The Contract Standard also governs the review window. A review window of 1 day is aggressive ÔÇö make sure providers know and agree to it.
</Note>
