## The Accepted Proposal

Once a buyer accepts your proposal, you'll receive a DM and the engagement appears in your **Work Panel**. The escrow is now locked — the buyer's funds are held until they release payment or the review window expires.

Open the workspace for the engagement to see:
- The full contract terms and Contract Standard
- Agreed deliverables, acceptance criteria, and deadline
- A place to submit your proof of work

---

## Submitting Deliverables

<Warning>
  Deliverables submitted through Jobly's formal deliverables system are the **only proof of work recognized in a dispute**. Work discussed over messages, shared in email, or delivered off-platform will not be considered by the AI arbitrator. Always submit formally.
</Warning>

You can submit deliverables multiple times — each submission overwrites the previous one. Submit early and update as work progresses.

### What to Include

**Description**
Write a clear summary of what was delivered. Reference the specific deliverables listed in the contract. Explain any decisions or deviations.

**URLs**
Link to everything relevant:
- GitHub/GitLab repositories
- Deployed applications or staging URLs
- Figma, Notion, or Google Drive files
- Live websites, APIs, or documentation

**Files**
Upload any files that need to be transferred — design assets, documents, data exports, etc.

<Tip>
  Make your deliverable submission self-contained. Imagine a third party with no context reading it — they should be able to verify every acceptance criterion from what you've submitted.
</Tip>

## Marking Work Complete

Once you've submitted your deliverables and the work is done:

1. Open the workspace for the engagement
2. Mark the work as complete

This triggers:
- Proposal status → `completed`
- The buyer's review window starts
- Buyer receives a notification with the deadline

The buyer now has their contracted review window to release payment or raise a dispute. After that deadline, payment **auto-releases to you** regardless.

---

## Getting Paid

Payment is released in one of three ways:

| How | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Buyer releases manually** | Buyer is satisfied — payment transfers immediately |
| **Auto-release** | Review window expires with no action — payment transfers automatically |
| **Dispute resolved in your favor** | Community or AI rules you won — escrowed funds release to you |

When payment releases:
- The full agreed price transfers to your JOOBs balance
- Your 10% bond from the proposal is also returned
- The engagement is complete

---

## If the Buyer Raises a Dispute

If the buyer believes the work doesn't meet the contract terms, they can raise a dispute. Funds are frozen while the dispute is active.

**What the AI arbitrator looks at:**
1. The final Contract Standard (deliverables, acceptance criteria)
2. What you formally submitted via the deliverables system
3. The message history between you and the buyer
4. The buyer's written reason for the dispute

**How to protect yourself:**
- Always formally submit deliverables — never just send files over chat
- Make sure your submission addresses every deliverable and acceptance criterion listed in the contract
- Keep communication in the Jobly messaging system so there's a record
- If you deviate from scope for good reason, document it in your deliverable description

If the AI verdict goes against you, you can **appeal** (costs 100 JOOBs) to escalate to a public community vote.

See [Dispute Resolution](/features/disputes) for the full process.
