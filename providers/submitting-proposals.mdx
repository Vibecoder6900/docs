## Finding Contracts

Browse open contracts at **Browse → Contracts**. You can filter by:
- **Category** — Tech, Design, Marketing, Legal, etc.
- **Status** — Open (actively accepting proposals) or Fulfilled
- **Skills** — Contracts requiring skills you have

Click any contract to see the full details — description, required skills, pricing terms, location, deadline, and the complete Contract Standard.

---

## Before You Propose

Read the entire contract carefully, especially the **Contract Standard**:

- **Deliverables** — Can you deliver every item listed?
- **Acceptance criteria** — Are the pass/fail conditions achievable?
- **Deadline** — Is it realistic for your schedule?
- **Review window** — How long does the buyer have to review before funds auto-release?
- **Conditions** — Any unusual requirements (NDA, in-person, specific tools)?

<Warning>
  The Contract Standard is what you'll be held to. If you accept a contract with strict acceptance criteria and miss one, expect a dispute. Make sure you're comfortable with every term before proposing.
</Warning>

## Submitting Your Proposal

When you're ready to propose:

**Hours / Price**
- For hourly contracts: enter the number of hours you'll deliver and your proposed hourly rate in JOOBs
- For fixed-price contracts: enter the total price in JOOBs

**Message**
Your pitch to the buyer. This is your chance to:
- Show you've read and understood the requirements
- Explain your approach and relevant experience
- Address any ambiguities upfront
- Set expectations on timeline and communication

**Conditions**
Your standard terms. These auto-populate from your profile if you link one. Add any contract-specific conditions here.

**Profile**
Optionally link one of your provider profiles to back the proposal. This gives buyers a quick view of your skills, rate, and background.

<Info>
  Submitting a proposal locks a **10% bond** from your JOOBs balance. This bond is refunded if your proposal is declined or withdrawn, and is kept if you abandon an accepted engagement.
</Info>

---

## After Submitting

Once submitted, your proposal is in `pending` status. The buyer will:
- Accept it (locking their funds and starting the engagement)
- Decline it (your bond is immediately refunded)
- Counter-offer (entering a negotiation)

You'll receive a direct message for each action.

---

## Negotiation

If the buyer counters your proposal, you'll receive a DM with their revised terms. You can:
- **Accept their counter** — engagement starts
- **Counter back** — propose yet another revision
- **Withdraw** — exit the negotiation; your bond is refunded

<Tip>
  Counter-offers are normal. Most engagements involve at least one round of negotiation. Be flexible on rate and hours if you want the contract — but don't compromise on deliverables that aren't feasible.
</Tip>

---

## Withdrawing a Proposal

You can withdraw a proposal at any time while it's `pending` or `countered`. Your 10% bond is refunded immediately.

You **cannot** withdraw after the buyer has accepted your proposal.
