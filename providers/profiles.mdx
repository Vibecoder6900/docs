## Why You Need a Profile

Your provider profile is your public presence on Jobly. Buyers browse profiles when evaluating proposals, and a strong profile builds credibility. It describes what you do, what you charge, where you're based, and what your standard conditions are.

You can also reference your profile when submitting proposals, making it easy for buyers to understand your background at a glance.

---

## Profile Fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Your headline (e.g. "Senior Full-Stack Engineer", "Brand Identity Designer") |
| **Description** | A full description of your services, experience, and approach |
| **Category** | Your primary discipline — Tech, Design, Marketing, Legal, etc. |
| **Skills** | The specific skills you offer (e.g. React, TypeScript, Figma, SEO) |
| **Hourly Rate** | Your base rate in JOOBs per hour |
| **Location** | "Remote" or your physical location |
| **Service Radius** | For in-person work, how far you'll travel (miles) — leave blank for remote/worldwide |
| **Capacity Hours** | How many hours of work you have available |
| **Standard Conditions** | Your default terms that apply to all engagements (e.g. "NDA required", "50% upfront", "No rush fees under 48 hours") |

---

## Writing a Strong Profile

**Title:** Be specific. "React + Node.js Developer" is stronger than "Software Engineer". Buyers filter by skill, so match your title to what you actually do.

**Description:** Cover:
- What types of work you specialize in
- Relevant experience or background
- How you work (communication style, revision policy, etc.)
- Any tools or frameworks you're expert in

**Skills:** Add every relevant skill — buyers filter by skill when browsing. Don't leave obvious ones out.

**Standard Conditions:** Use this to set expectations up front. If you always require a deposit, put it here. If you don't work weekends, say so. Clear conditions prevent disputes later.

<Tip>
  Conditions in your profile carry over to proposals you submit. Buyers see them before they accept. There are no surprises.
</Tip>

---

## Profile Status

| Status | Visibility | Proposals |
|---|---|---|
| `active` | Visible in Browse | Accepting new proposals |
| `paused` | Hidden from Browse | Existing proposals continue |
| `closed` | Hidden from Browse | No new proposals |

Pause your profile when you're at capacity. Close it when you're winding down.

---

## Reviews on Your Profile

After completing engagements, buyers can leave reviews on your profile (1–5 stars + written feedback). Your average star rating is displayed publicly and helps buyers evaluate you.

The best way to earn good reviews is to deliver exactly what the Contract Standard describes — no more, no less — and communicate clearly throughout the engagement.
